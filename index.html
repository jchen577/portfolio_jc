Woofmart

Play as a energetic and excited dog, ready to run rampant on an unsuspecting store! Knock down items to rack up points while evading the supermarket workers!

In this game, I worked on the physics interactions. This entails resizing the player character and adjusting their mass to change how they push objects around. I also worked on making sure that objects pushed would react appropriately, and that fallin on the floor would destroy them and award the player points

Dicey Decisions

A representation of how children see the world of Dungeons and Dragons! Roll dice, move your piece, collect resources, play cards, all in an attempt to gather reputation. Beat your rival to the reputation goal and win!

I worked on nearly all parts of this game. My biggest focus was on the dice, ensuring they randomly rolled, were able to be selected, and creating backend support for other developers to use their information. My 2nd biggest contribution is working on the game manager, making sure that players couldn't perform actions outside of the rules, and combining other mechanics made by the other developers.

RPG

A standard RPG where you can fight enemies, get stronger, and acquires riches!

This game is coded in Phaser, and was a self-practice project where I would take the concepts I learned in class and implement them. Some key features are the self-made inventory system and a WIP A* pathfinding algorithm. The game has a lot of mechanics/features of a typical RPG such as experience, coins, shop NPCs, and level-locked areas. In the future I plan to add more content in the forms of items, enemies, and map expansions.
